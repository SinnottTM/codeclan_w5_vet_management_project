Facts Page

  • Kaiju is a Japanese word meaning "strange creature"
  • Godzilla's original name, Gojira, was rumored to be the nickname of a tough guy at Toho Studios.
  • Godzilla's classic roar is a surprising mix of sounds, including a leather glove being rubbed against a double bass
  • Godzilla was originally going to be a giant, mutated octopus.
  • Godzilla went head-to-head with basketball legend Charles Barkley in a comic once. It was very silly
  • The name Godzilla is a transliteration of “Gojira,” a combination of two Japanese words: "gorira," meaning gorilla; and "kujira," meaning whale.
  • In real life, the marine activism group, Sea Shepherd Conservation Society, named a high-tech, $4 million vessel “Gojira” in honor of the Godzilla character; the vessel was used in the campaign against Japanese whalers in the Southern Ocean Whale Sanctuary.
  • For his 50th birthday (of sorts) in 2004, Godzilla was given a star on Hollywood's Walk of Fame.
  • A Godzilla suit was stolen, then lost, then randomly washed ashore. One of the monster’s costumes was stolen from a Toho garage, only to be found washed up on the shores of Lake Okutama near Tokyo.
